About Me

I have a passion for machine learning, coding, and AI.

In 2020 I earned a Bachelor of Science degree in Computer Engineering Technology from the Rochester Institute of Technology. During that time I taught myself everything I could about machine learning and was fortunate enough to be accepted for an internship at the Center for Systems Biology, a multi-discilinary bio-technology research lab at the Massachusetts General Hospital. There I developed novel image processing techniques based in machine learning to identify potentially cancerous cells in images of tissue samples taken with digital holography.
Since then I have been pursuing research in reinforcement learning as a researcher at the same lab. My work has involved tackling fundamental questions about the nature of exploration in decision processes, which led to a novel mathematical framework about agent behavior, and a highly scalable finite differences algorithm for deep reinforcement learning. I am currently investigating improvements to modern off-policy learning algorithms.

Accomplishments

I've spent my life working with computers and code. Here are a few of my proudest accomplishements.

Real-time Low-cost Lymphoma & Breast Cancer Detection

I designed, developed, and tested deep learning image processing methods to automatically detect and classify potentially cancerous cells in real-time from a digital hologram. I developed a GUI for technicians to interact with my code and deployed it on a Raspberry Pi. This technology is currently used in Botswana to provide low-cost cancer screenings for people who may otherwise be unable to afford them. The development of this technology led to two publications in Nature and ACS Nano

Rocket League Gym

My passion for gaming and reinforcement learning led me to co-author a mod and API for the popular Esport Rocket League which enables AI practitioners to control the game via Python as though it were an Gymnasium-style learning environment. We titled this project the Rocket League Gym (RLGym), and it has gained a large following of interested bot-makers. Users of our project came together to develop a high-level open-source bot called Nexto with reinforcement learning. This bot was trained with compute power that was crowd-sourced from our users and became so good at the game that it caught the eye of several popular YouTube content creators like Sunless Khan, Lethamyr, Rocket Sledge, and more. Across all videos about Nexto, the RLGym community bot has accumulated over 1 million total views.

A Scalable Finite Difference Method for Deep Reinforcement Learning

In my first-author work, I developed a highly scalable distributed system to train reinforcement learning agents with a novel finite differences algorithm. The architecture of the platform uses gRPC to facilitate communication between a central learning process and any number of connected remote worker processes. The system is fully deterministic and natively integrated with Weights & Biases to support hyperparameter sweeps and repeatable experiments. My paper about this project can be found here and some starter code which includes both the scalable finite differences algorithm and an implementation of the generalized Novelty Search algorithm from my prior research can be found on my GitHub here.
