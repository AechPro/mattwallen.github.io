About Me

I have a passion for Machine Learning, coding, and AI.

In 2020 I earned a Bachelor of Science degree in Computer Engineering Technology from the Rochester Institute of Technology. During that time I taught myself everything I could about Machine Learning, and was fortunate enough to be accepted for an internship at the Center for Systems Biology, a multi-discilinary bio-technology research lab at the Massachusetts General Hospital. There I developed novel image processing techniques to identify potentially cancerous cells in images of tissue samples taken with digital holography.
Since then I have been pursuing a first author work in Reinforcement Learning where I am tackling fundamental questions pertaining to the nature of exploration. This work has already lead to two pre-publications on arXiv that are available here and here.

Accomplishments

I've spent my life working with computers and code. Here are a few of my proudest accomplishements.

Real-time Low-cost Lymphoma & Breast Cancer Detection

I designed, developed, and tested deep learning image processing methods to automatically detect and classify potentially cancerous cells in real-time from a digital hologram. I developed a GUI for technicians to interact with my code and deployed it on a Raspberry Pi. This technology is currently used in Botswana to provide low-cost cancer screenings for people who may otherwise be unable to afford them. The development of this technology led to two publications in Nature and ACS Nano

Rocket League Gym

My passion for gaming and Reinforcement Learning led me to co-author a mod and API for the popular Esport Rocket League which enables AI practitioners to control the game via Python as though it were an Openai Gym-style learning environment. We titled this project the Rocket League Gym (RLGym), and it has gained enough of a following that a few users of our project developed a high-level community-based bot called Nexto with Reinforcement Learning. This bot was trained with compute power that was crowd-sourced from our users and became so good at the game that it caught the eye of several popular YouTube content creators like Sunless Khan, Lethamyr, Rocket Sledge, and more. Across all videos about Nexto, the RLGym community bot has accumulated over 1 million total views.

Distributable Reinforcement Learning

I have developed multiple large-scale distributable computing platforms for Reinforcement Learning. While the first is still undergoing private development for a future publication, the second is publicly available on my GitHub and provides a variety of useful tools for scientific computing. The architecture of the platform enables multiple asynchronous machines to transmit data to Redis, where a learning algorithm can consume that data and compute updates to a policy.
